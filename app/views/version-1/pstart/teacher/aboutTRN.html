Teacher reference number

If you don’t know what your teacher reference number (TRN) is, this can usually be found on your payslip, teachers’ pension documentation or teacher training records.

The TRN is typically seven digits long, but previously has been known as a QTS, GTC, DfE, DfES or DCSF number and therefore may include the letters RP and the forward slash symbol ‘/’. For example: 4567814 or 95/78674

If you cannot find your TRN, you can request a reminder.

Request a TRN reminder by email

Send an email qts.enquiries@education.gov.uk which includes your:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • National Insurance number

Request a TRN reminder by phone

Call the Teaching Regulation Agency on 020 7593 5394
Make sure you have your National Insurance number ready before you call.
