Getting your teacher reference number

If you don’t know what your teacher reference number (TRN) is, you can find this on your payslip, teachers’ pensions documentation or teacher training records.

The TRN is typically seven digits long, but previously has been known as a QTS, GTC, DfE, DfES or DCSF number and therefore may include the letters RP and the forward slash symbol ‘/’. For example: 4567814 or 95/78674

Request a TRN reminder by email

Send an email to qts.enquiries@education.gov.uk. Put the phrase ‘Undertaking an NPQ and require TRN clarification’ in the subject line. The email needs to include your:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • National Insurance number

The team aim to respond to valid email requests within 5 working days.

Request a TRN reminder by phone

Call the Teaching Regulation Agency on 020 7593 5394.

Make sure you have your National Insurance number ready before you call.

Phone lines are open 9 AM – 5PM Monday – Thursday and 9AM – 4:30 PM on Friday. Lines are closed on bank holidays.

State that you are studying for a National Professional Qualification and need a reminder of your TRN.
