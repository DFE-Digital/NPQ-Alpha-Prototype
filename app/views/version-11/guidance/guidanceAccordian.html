Take a national professional qualification (NPQ)

A National Professional Qualification (NPQ) is one of a series of leadership qualifications available to qualified teachers.

Each is designed to equip teachers with the skills and knowledge to make a big impact at the next stage of their career, as well as providing the basis to make improvements across their current school.

From September 2021, the existing four NPQs will be replaced by six new NPQs, designed to reform the qualifications first established in the 1990s.

Why should I study for an NPQ

Studying for an NPQ is a major step forward in your career’s development.

The skills you learn during the NPQ you choose will benefit your own career and the teaching environment in which you work.

Benefits for schools include:

  • an increase in workforce capacity and individual capabilities
  • successful retention of staff
  • knowledge-based experience for the staffroom

What courses are available

From September 2021, NPQ courses previously will be changed.

These changes include:

  • replacing the existing NPQs in senior leadership, headship and executive leadership
  • replacing the current NPQ in middle leadership with 3 new NPQs for teachers and leaders who want to develop their expertise in specialist areas of teaching practice:
    • supporting the training and development of others
    • developing teachers who are subject leads or responsible for improving teaching practice in a subject or phase
    • developing teachers who have responsibilities for leading behaviour and culture
  • knowledge-based experience for the staffroom

These will be known as:

  • NPQ Senior Leadership
  • NPQ Executive Leadership
  • NPQ Headship
  • NPQ Leading Teaching Development
  • NPQ Leading Teaching
  • NPW Leading Behaviour and Culture

Who will pay for my NPQ

Candidates from schools that sit within a Category 5 or Category 6 area are eligible for fully funded DfE scholarships.

Candidates from schools within MATs or dioceses where at least one school sits within a Category 5 or Category 6 area are also eligible.

How do I find a provider

There are 18 training providers across the United Kingdom which run training courses.

Some providers may have specific criteria depending on your school’s circumstances.

For example, the provider may require your school to be rated ‘good’ or ‘outstanding’ if you wish

How do I apply

You should check with your school’s qualification officer regarding eligibility for your chosen course.
